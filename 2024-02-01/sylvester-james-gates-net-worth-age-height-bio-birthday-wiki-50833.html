Sylvester James Gates Biography

Sylvester James Gates is one of the most popular and richest Physicist who was born on December 15, 1950 in Tampa, Florida, United States. Sylvester James Gates Jr. (born on December 15th 1950) who is popularly referred to in the media as S. James Gates Jr. or Jim Gates, is an American theoretical physicist, who studies in the fields of supersymmetry, supergravity and the theory of superstrings. He has retired from the Physics Department and the Center for Fundamental Physics at the University of Maryland College of Computer, Mathematical, and Natural Sciences in the year 2017 and is currently in the position of Brown Theoretical Physics Center Director and The Ford Foundation Professor of Physics and Mathematics, as well as an Affiliate Professor and Watson Institute for International Studies & Public Affairs Faculty Fellow at Brown University. He was an University of Maryland Regents Professor and was a member of former President Obama’s Council of Advisors on Science and Technology.

On February 1st, 2013 Gates was awarded the National Medal of Science. Gates has been elected into the National Academy of Sciences in 2013.

Gates was nominated by the Department of Energy as one of the USA Science and Engineering Festival’s “Nifty Fifty” Speakers to present his work and career to middle and high school students in October 2010. On December 5, 2016, Gates spoke at the 2016 Quadrennial Physics Congress, the largest ever gathering of physics undergraduates.

In 2018, Gates was elected to the presidential line of the American Physical Society: he began serving as its Vice President in 2019, and will serve as President as of 2021.

Gates was a Martin Luther King Jr. Visiting Scholar at MIT (2010-11) and was a Residential Scholar at MIT’s Simmons Hall. He is currently conducting research on supersymmetry, string theory as well as supergravity, and supergravity at Brown University. His current research focuses on Adinkra symbols, which is a graph-theoretic method to study the supersymmetric theory of representation.

Gates, the oldest of four siblings, was born in Tampa, Florida, the son of Sylvester James Gates, Sr., a career U.S. Army man, and Charlie Engels Gates. His mother died when he was 11. When his father remarried, his new stepmother, a teacher, brought books into the home and emphasized the importance of education. The family moved many times while Gates was growing up, but, as he was entering 11th grade, settled in Orlando, Florida, where James attended Jones High School—his first experience in a segregated African-American school. Comparing his own school’s quality to neighboring white schools, “I understood pretty quickly that the cards were really stacked against us.” Nevertheless, an 11th grade course in physics established Gates’ career interest in that field, especially its mathematical side.     Why is supersymmetry needed?

    Supersymmetry is an extension of the Standard Model that aims to fill some of the gaps. It predicts a partner particle for each particle in the Standard Model. These new particles would solve a major problem with the Standard Model – fixing the mass of the Higgs boson.
