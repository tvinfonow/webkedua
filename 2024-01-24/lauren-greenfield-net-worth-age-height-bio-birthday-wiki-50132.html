Lauren Greenfield (born June 28 in 1966) is an American artist documentarian, documentary photographer, as well as a documentary film maker. She has released four photographic monographs and directed four documentary films and produced four traveling exhibits and was published in magazines across the globe.

Greenfield’s thesis from her undergraduate degree helped get her started as an intern with National Geographic Magazine. An additional award of National Geographic provided financial support for her debut work, “Fast Forward: Growing Up in the Shadow of Hollywood” (Knopf 1997). After five years of the publication of “Fast Forward”, Greenfield published a new major collection of work on the crisis of self-esteem among American women, which she titled “Girl Culture”.

Her third major exhibition, THIN, accompanied both a feature-length documentary film, Thin (HBO, 2006), and a published photographic book, THIN (Chronicle Books, 2006). The exhibition debuted at The Women’s Museum in Dallas, Texas and continued to exhibit through 2010.

Greenfield subsequently directed THIN, a feature-length documentary for HBO, and published an accompanying book with the same title. THIN was selected for competition at the Sundance Film Festival in 2006. In September 2006, Greenfield received the prestigious Grierson Award for best documentary shown at the BFI London Film Festival. It also won the Grand Jury Prize at the Independent Film Festival of Boston, the Newport International Film Festival, and the Jackson Hole Film Festival. Greenfield received a 2007 Emmy nomination for Outstanding Directing For Nonfiction Programming for her work on THIN.

Alongside the release of her first monograph, Fast Forward: Growing up in the shadow of Hollywood (Knopf 1997) Her first major exhibition, “Fast Forward” had its US debut at the International Center for Photography (ICP) on April 25, 1997. It was extended for two more months due to the huge attendance and high acclaim from critics (April 25 to September 7 1997). The show was exhibited in France and The Netherlands, Italy, Russia as well as a variety of cultural venues across North America.

From September 1985 until May 1986, when she was still an undergraduate student at Harvard, Greenfield traveled around the globe in a nine-month program developed in the International Honors Program, entitled “Film Study and Anthropology.” The program introduced her to documentary and anthropological production across France, Yugoslavia, Hungary, Austria, India, Australia and Japan. In a 2012 interview , Sara Melson (for Harvardwood.com), Greenfield was quoted as she said “We watched many indigenous films, and we met with amazing directors. It was on that trip that I realized my calling. I wasn’t sure if it would be sociology, film, photography, or anthropology, but looking at culture was my calling. When I got back to Harvard, I switched my major from Social Studies to Visual Studies. I soon realized that theory wasn’t my medium, and I moved toward filmmaking and photography.” In Harvard, Greenfield continued her filmmaking classes under the guidance of veteran documentarian Robb Moss. She graduated in 1988 and was a co-director of the 25-minute film Once You’re in, about Irish illegal immigrants who resided in Boston.

Greenfield earned her degree from Harvard University in 1987 with the degree of B.A., majoring in Visual and Environmental Studies. Her senior thesis photo project focused on the French Aristocracy was titled “Survivors of the French Revolution”.
