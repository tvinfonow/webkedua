What was Julio Cortázar known for?

Julio Cortázar, also known as Julio Denis, was born in 1914 and passed away in 1984. He was an Argentine short-story writer and novelist who combined experimental writing techniques and existential questioning in his work.

What is Julio Cortázar most famous work?

Cortázar’s masterpiece, Rayuela (1963; Hopscotch), is an open-ended novel, or antinovel; the reader is invited to rearrange the different parts of the novel according to a plan prescribed by the author. It was the first of the “boom” of Latin American novels of the 1960s to gain international attention.

What was Julio Cortázar style of writing?

His literary work focuses on poetry and short stories that often treat elements of fantasy. Cortázar was also very vocal about his political opinions. He fiercely opposed the government of Juan Peron, for which he served a short time in prison. His anti-peronist views prompted him to move to Paris in 1951.
