Michael Chertoff, born November 28, 1953, is an American lawyer. He was the second Secretary of Homeland Security in the United States. He is the co-author the USA PATRIOT Act. He was previously a United States Circuit Judge of United States Court of Appeals, the Third Circuit. On February 15, 2005, he succeeded Tom Ridge to the U.S. Department of Homeland Security.

Chertoff was a student at the Elizabeth Jewish Educational Center and the Pingry School. In 1975, he graduated from Harvard College with his Bachelor of Arts degree. He spent his second year abroad at the London School of Economics and Political Science. After graduating from Harvard Law School, he was a research assistant to John Hart Ely. He worked on the book Democracy and Distrust. Chertoff was awarded a Juris Doctor magna laude in 1978. He then worked as a law clerk for Judge Murray Gurfein of The United States Court of Appeals, Second Circuit, and later, for Justice William J. Brennan, Jr., of the United States Supreme Court, from 1979 to 1980.

In 1990, Chertoff was appointed by President George H. W. Bush as United States Attorney for the District of New Jersey. Among his most important cases, in 1992 Chertoff achieved conviction of second-term Jersey City mayor Gerald McCann on charges of defrauding money from a savings and loan scam. McCann served two years in federal prison.
