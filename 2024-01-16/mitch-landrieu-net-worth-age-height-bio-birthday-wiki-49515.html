Mitchell Joseph Landrieu (/’ l aen d ru:/ LAN-droo) is an American politician and attorney. He was Mayor of New Orleans between 2010 and 2018. Landrieu, a Democrat, was Lieutenant Governor of Louisiana between 2004 and 2010.

Mitch Landrieu was elected Lieutenant Governor in 2003. This was his first attempt at statewide office in Louisiana. Landrieu was elected Lieutenant Governor after 16 years of service in the State House. Landrieu won the Louisiana open primary with 53 percent of the vote. This result prevented him from being elected to the general election. Three Republicans were Landrieu’s main opponents: former U.S. Representative Clyde C. Holloway from Rapides Parish and former Lieutenant Governor Melinda Schwegmann, both of New Orleans. Kirt Bennett, a Baton Rouge businessman, was also present.

When Hurricanes Katrina and Rita hit New Orleans and the Gulf Coast in 2005, the Lieutenant Governor was involved in the emergency response. After commanding portions of the hurricane response and recovery, Landrieu was tasked to lead the effort to rebuild the state’s tourism industry.

In February 2006, Landrieu officially announced he would run for mayor of New Orleans in the April 22 election. Before Hurricane Katrina the incumbent Ray Nagin was widely expected to be reelected with little difficulty, but post-disaster problems and controversies had left many New Orleanians interested in new leadership.

Landrieu unsuccessfully tried for the office as Mayor of New Orleans in 1994. Marc Morial, a son of a former mayor, took the office.
