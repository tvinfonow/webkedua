Luke Willson Biography

Luke Willson is one of the most popular and richest American football tight end who was born on January 15, 1990 in LaSalle, Quebec, Canada. Luke Michael Willson, a Canadian professional American football tight-end for the Seattle Seahawks (NFL), was born January 15, 1990. The Seahawks selected him in the fifth round, 2013 NFL Draft from Rice University. He was also selected by the Toronto Argonauts during the 2012 CFL Draft but didn’t sign. He has also played with the Detroit Lions.

His sophomore season 2010 was his best with 33 receptions, 425 receiving yardage, and 3 receiving touchdowns.

Willson finished his senior season in 2012 with a total of nine receptions for 126 receiving yards and two receiving touchdowns, which earned him a spot in the NFLPA Collegiate Bowl.

In that season’s playoffs, Willson caught four passes for 68 yards and a touchdown in a divisional round game against the Carolina Panthers. A week later, he caught a key two-point conversion in the NFC Championship Game against the Green Bay Packers, as the Seahawks rallied to defeat the Packers 28–22 to reached consecutive Super Bowls. In Super Bowl XLIX, Willson had no catches as the Seahawks failed to repeat as champions, defeated 28–24 by the New England Patriots, who rallied in the fourth quarter behind quarterback Tom Brady.

From 2009 to 2012, Willson was a Rice University tight end. David Sloan, a former tight end for the Detroit Lions, was Willson’s position coach at Rice. Willson was late reporting to camp 2008 because of his Canadian baseball commitments, so he redshirted as an actual freshman.
