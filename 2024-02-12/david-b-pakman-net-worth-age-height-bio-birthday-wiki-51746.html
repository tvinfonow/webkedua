David B. Pakman Biography

David B. Pakman is one of the most popular and richest Businessperson who was born on January 29, 1969 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, United States. David Pakman (born January 29 1969, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania) is an internet-based investor and entrepreneur. As Venrock’s Partner in its New York City office, David concentrates specifically on the initial stages of investment in enterprise and consumer internet-related businesses. His investments in active comprise Dstillery (formerly Media6Degrees), Smartling, YouNow, Misty Robotics, Simbe Robotics, Dapper Labs/CryptoKitties as well as Amino. He was the leader of both the Series B and A rounds as well as was as a member of the Board for Dollar Shave Club which was purchased from Unilever at a price of $1billion. He was a member of the board of Crunchyroll (acquired from The Chernin Group) and directed Venrock’s investment in Klout (acquired from Lithium Technologies).

The year 2003 saw Pakman was recruited by Dimensional Associates, a private equity company which focuses on buying distressed digital media businesses. The company purchased eMusic by Vivendi Universal, and Pakman was appointed COO. The year 2005 saw Pakman took over as CEO. He expanded eMusic to be, at the time the leading global retailer of music on the internet and music, just behind iTunes in terms of the number in downloads purchased. In the three years Pakman managed the business and grew the company by over 850%, going from just $7 million in revenue to over $68 million. Pakman transformed the company from an unassuming and insignificant company with 50,000 users to become the second-largest digital retailer of music in the world boasting more than 400 000 customers as well as more than 12 percent of the market (according an analysis by the NPD Group).

On November 29, 2012, Pakman testified in front of the U. S. House Judiciary Committee Subcommittee on Intellectual Property, Competition and the Internet regarding the state of internet radio licensing.

Pakman also serves on the Board of Trustees of ITHAKA, a not-for-profit organization dedicated to helping the academic community take full advantage of rapidly advancing information and networking technologies (see JSTOR) and holds a board seat with the New York Venture Capital Association. In September 2012, Pakman joined Jazz at Lincoln Center’s Digital Advisory Council. Pakman is a Board Member of POTS (Part Of The Solution), a “One-Stop-Shop” in the Bronx helping low-income individuals and families move from crisis to stability, and ultimately self-sufficiency. In June 2015, Pakman became a Trustee at St. Luke’s School (Connecticut), a secular, college-preparatory day school in New Canaan, Connecticut for grades 5 through 12.

Before joining eMusic, Pakman was co-founder and the president of public and business development government policy for Myplay, Inc., the company he co-founded in the year 1999 with Doug Camplejohn in Redwood City, California with Doug Camplejohn. The company created”the “digital music locker” and established the locker category as the first of its kind. The company was sold in 2001. Myplay was acquired by Bertelsmann’s ecommerce group, where Pakman was promoted to SVP for Corporate Development and Public Policy. Prior to Myplay was acquired, he served as vice president of N2K Entertainment, which created the first ever digital music download service.
