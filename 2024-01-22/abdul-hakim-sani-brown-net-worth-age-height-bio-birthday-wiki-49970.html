Abdul Hakim Sani Brown (サニブラウン・アブデル・ハキーム , Saniburaun Abuderu Hakīmu, born 6 March 1999) is a Japanese athlete specialising in sprinting events. Sani Brown has a Japanese mother and a Ghanaian father. Sani Brown won the 100 metres at the 2015 World Youth Championships in Athletics setting a championship record of 10.28 (−0.4) in the final.

In Japan, Sani Brown ran for Josai High School, a private high school operated by the same corporation that operates Josai University in the Toshima ward of Tokyo. and qualified for the 2020 Tokyo Olympic Summer Games running 100 in 9.99 seconds on 12 May 2019.

In 2016, he committed to the University of Florida. In the 2019 NCAA championships he set the Japanese record at 9.97s.
