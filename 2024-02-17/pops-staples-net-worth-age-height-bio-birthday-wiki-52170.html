Top Facts about Pops Staples

  • Pops Staples was born in Mississippi in 1914.
  • He formed The Staple Singers with his family in 1948.
  • The group’s hit songs include “I’ll Take You There” and “Respect Yourself.”
  • Pops Staples was a civil rights activist and friend of Martin Luther King Jr.
  • He received a Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award in 2005.
  • Pops Staples died in Chicago at the age of 85.
  • His music influenced artists such as Bob Dylan and Prince.
  • He played guitar for Mavis Staples’ solo career after The Staple Singers disbanded.
  • Pops Staples was posthumously inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1999.
  • His final album, “Don’t Lose This,” was released posthumously in 2015, produced by Jeff Tweedy of Wilco fame.
