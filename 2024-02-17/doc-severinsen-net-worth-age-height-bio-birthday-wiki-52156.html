Top Facts about Doc Severinsen

1. Carl Hilding “Doc” Severinsen (born July 7 1927) is an American retired jazz trumpeter.
2. He was nicknamed Doc after his father, who was a dentist in Arlington, Oregon.
3. Severinsen won a state trumpet contest at age 9.
4. He became the leader of the NBC Orchestra on The Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson.
5. He substituted for Ed McMahon on occasions when Ed was absent.
6. Severinsen released an album with the jazz fusion group Xebron in 1985.
7. He won the Grammy Award for Best Large Jazz Ensemble with his album The Tonight Show Band with Doc Severinsen.
8. He performed the “Star-Spangled Banner” on at least three nationally telecast occasions.
9. Severinsen is credited for co-writing the hit song “Stop and Smell the Roses” with Mac Davis.
10. Severinsen held positions as the principal pops conductor for several orchestras in the US.
11. He retired from conducting in 2007.
12. Severinsen was inducted into the Scandinavian-American Hall of Fame in 2014.
13. He has been married four times.
14. His current partner, Cathy Leach, is a professor emerita of trumpet at the University of Tennessee.
15. Severinsen performed his final concert in Saratoga Springs, New York, on September 1 2022.
