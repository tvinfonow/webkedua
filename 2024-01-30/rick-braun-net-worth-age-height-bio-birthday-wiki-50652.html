Rick Braun Biography

Rick Braun is one of the most popular and richest Trumpeter who was born on July 6, 1955 in Allentown, Pennsylvania, United States. Rick Braun was born July 6, 1955 and is a smooth jazz trumpeter as well as a vocalist, record producer, and singer.

He and Richard Elliot, saxophonist, co-founded ARTizen Music Group (now Artistry Music Group) in 2005. Rykodisc was once their distributor.

He achieved several top chartings including Kisses in the Rain (as high as number 1), R n R (as high as number 1), All It Takes (as high as number 2), and Can You Feel It (as high as number 1) along with charting at the Traditional Jazz Albums for the first time in 2011 with the vocal album Sings with Strings (as high as number 9).

Intimate secrets was his first solo album. Night Walk followed in 1992. Christmas Present was also released. Beat Street was his 1995 debut album, which saw him gain enough popularity to decide to go solo.

Rick Braun Net Worth

Rick Braun is one of the richest Trumpeter from United States. According to our analysis, Wikipedia, Forbes & Business Insider, Rick Braun's net worth $5 Million. (Last Update: December 11, 2023)

Braun’s mother was a self taught pianist and banjoist. Braun was a drummer in high school and then he followed his brother to the trumpet. He attended the Eastman School of Music in the 1970s. While there, he joined Auracle, a jazz-fusion group. Braun was the co-producer of the second album, which was produced by Teo Macero.

He entered pop music in the 1980s. He was a Japanese singer who released an album and then became a songwriter at Lorimar (Warner Chappell). He co-wrote the hit song “Here with Me”, which was a top 20 hit for REO Speedwagon. He returned to the trumpet and worked as a studio musician, touring musician, and with Crowded House and Natalie Cole.

He has cited as influences Miles Davis, Lee Morgan, Chet Baker, Clark Terry, Dizzy Gillespie and Herb Alpert, with the last one inspiring his album All It Takes including a song called “Tijuana Dance” (a play on Alpert’s band Tijuana Brass). One of his influences was Freddie Hubbard, and Braun composed a song, “Freddie Was Here” in 2008, which he recorded on his album, All it Takes, in tribute to Hubbard, who died that year.
