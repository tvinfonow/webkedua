What is the meaning of Maximilian?

Maximilian is a male-given name of Latin origin. Sharing its meaning to variants “Maxwell,” “Max,” and girl’s name “Maxine,” Maximilian translates to “greatest.” The name was popular among monarchs during the 15th and 16th centuries before making its way to the kings and queens of popular culture.

Where is the name Maximilian from?

Etymology. Saint’s name from Latin Maximilianus, a derivative of Maximus “the greatest”. It was used in German royal houses after Friedrich III chose it for his son in 1459, explaining it as a combination of the names of two Roman generals, Maximus and Aemilianus.
