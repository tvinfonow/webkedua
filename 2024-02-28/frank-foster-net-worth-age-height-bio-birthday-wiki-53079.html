Frank Foster Biography

Frank Foster is one of the most popular and richest Saxophonist who was born on September 23, 1928 in Cincinnati, Ohio, United States. Award-winning saxophonist, humanitarian, and leader of Living Color and The Loud Minority. He played often with Count Basie.

He arranged “Mack the Knife” for Frank Sinatra on L.A. Is My Lady.

He was married to Cecilia Foster, his wife of forty-five years; they raised six children.
