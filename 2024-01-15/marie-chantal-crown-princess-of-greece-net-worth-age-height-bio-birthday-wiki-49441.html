Marie-Chantal, Crown Princess of Greece Biography

Marie-Chantal, Crown Princess of Greece is one of the most popular and richest Businessperson who was born on September 17, 1968 in London, England, United Kingdom. Marie-Chantal, Crown Princess of Greece, Princess of Denmark (Marie-Chantal Claire; née Miller; born 17 September 1968) is the wife of Pavlos, Crown Prince of Greece, son of Constantine II of Greece and Anne-Marie of Denmark. Her husband is the heir apparent to the defunct throne of Greece, as the monarchy was abolished in 1973. She is a Danish princess by marriage, as her husband is a male-line descendant of Christian IX of Denmark.

In 2010, the British employment tribunal ordered Marie-Chantal Miller to pay a £17,500 compensation fine for illegal dismissal of her Filipino maid and for disregarding “the proper dismissal process”.

Marie-Chantal is the Founder and Creative Director of Marie-Chantal, an international childrenswear brand founded in 2000. She is a trustee of the Royal Academy Trust and a board director of DFS Group Ltd.
