Rodney Allison Biography

Rodney Allison is one of the most popular and richest Head Coach who was born on January 29, 1956 in Odessa, United States. Rodney Allison, born January 29, 1956, is an American ex-college football player and coach. He is currently the director for the Double T Varsity Club, a Texas Tech letterman’s club.

In 1979, Texas Tech hired Allison as a graduate assistant. Allison was promoted to running-backs coach in 1980, a position that he would continue to hold at many schools. Steve Sloan, Allison’s former Texas Tech coach, hired him as the Duke running backs coach.

In 2003 Sloan, then the athletic director at the University of Tennessee at Chattanooga, hired Allison as the head coach of the Chattanooga Mocs.

He was inducted into the Texas Tech Athletic Hall of Honor in 2003.

After graduating from Texas Tech in 1977, Allison was not selected by the National Football League (NFL). However, he went on to play for one year with the Toronto Argonauts (CFL) after his graduation. He ran for 49 yards in 13 of his 15 regular season games with the Argonauts during 1978.
