Jorik Scholten, Dutch pronunciation: [‘jorIk.sxolt@n]; was born 15 October 1994. He is also known professionally as Lil’ Kleine (pronounced “lIl.’klein@]). Ronnie Flex, a fellow rapper, produced the number-one single “Drank and Drugs” as well as the number-one album WOP!

Scholten signed early with talent agencies and made his acting debut in 2005’s Diep. He began performing on-stage with Lange Frans’ D-Men band after making friends with the rapper Lange Frans. He adopted the stage name of “Lil’ Kleine” in 2012 and released his first EP “Tuig van de Richel”. He recorded the song “Zo Verdomd Alleen” with Danny de Munk in 2012, and it reached the Single Top 100.

In 2017, Lil’ Kleine was one of fifteen celebrities who competed on the nineteenth season of Expeditie Robinson. He was the first contestant to leave the competition, having been voted off at his own request after three days.
