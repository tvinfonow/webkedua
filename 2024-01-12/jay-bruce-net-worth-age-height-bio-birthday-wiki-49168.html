Jay Bruce (born April 3, 1987) is an American professional baseball corner outfielder for the Philadelphia Phillies of Major League Baseball (MLB). He previously played for the Cincinnati Reds, New York Mets, Cleveland Indians, and Seattle Mariners. The Reds drafted Bruce in the first round, 12th overall pick, of the 2005 Major League Baseball draft; he made his MLB debut in 2008. Bruce has been named an All-Star three times during his career, and has won the Silver Slugger Award twice.

Bruce’s professional career began in 2005 in the Gulf Coast League, playing for the Reds’ short-season rookie team. He hit .270/.331/.500 in 122 at bats in 37 games before moving on to the Reds’ rookie affiliate Billings Mustangs. There he hit .257/.358/.457 with four home runs and 13 runs batted in (RBIs) in 70 at bats. Baseball America ranked him the top prospect in the Pioneer League, and the second-best prospect in the Gulf Coast League.
