Top Facts about Freddie Webb

1. Freddie Nelle Webb was born on November 24, 1942.
2. He completed his elementary education at San Beda College and his degree in Bachelor of Arts in English at Colegio de San Juan de Letran.
3. He is married to Elizabeth Pagaspas of Tanauan, Batangas and has six children including Hubert, Pinky, and Jason Webb.
4. Webb first played for the Letran Knights (1960-1964) and became a star for the team.
5. He later played for YCO Painters (1965-1975) and Tanduay Rhum Makers (1976-1978) in the MICAA and the PBA, respectively.
6. He was a member of the Philippine National Basketball Team in the 1972 Summer Olympics.
7. He coached YCO-Tanduay (1981-1983) and Shell (1985) in the PBA.
8. He was elected in 1971 as city councilor in Pasay, holding the post up to 1978.
9. He ran and won in the Philippine legislative elections in 1987, becoming the first representative of the lone district of Parañaque.
10. He held various chairmanships in the senate from 1992 to 1998, notably in the Senate Committees/Committee on Health and Demography and the Committee on Games and Amusements.
11. He sponsored the provisions creating the Sangguniang Kabataan in the Local Government Code of 1991.
12. He authored, co-authored and steered into law, acts such as the National Health Insurance Act, The Hepatitis-B Immunization Act, The Corneal Transplant Law, the Voluntary Blood Donation Act, and the Act Granting Benefits to Barangay Health Workers.
13. Webb began a career in movies, television, radio and sportscasting from his days as a basketball player.
14. He hosted the daily show Pa-bandying, Bandying (1973) over RPN and became a household name with the pairing of him with Nova Villa in the show Chicks to Chicks in 1979.
15. He was popularly known for the line “Sweetheart, ligo na tayo.”
