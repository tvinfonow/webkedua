Top Facts about Gianni Versace

  • Gianni Versace was born in Reggio Calabria, Italy in 1946.
  • He founded his eponymous fashion house in 1978.
  • Versace’s designs were known for their bold prints and bright colors.
  • The brand expanded to include fragrances, home decor, and accessories.
  • Versace dressed many celebrities including Princess Diana and Elton John.
  • He was murdered outside his Miami Beach mansion in 1997.
  • His sister Donatella took over as creative director of the brand.
  • The company went public on the Milan Stock Exchange in 1999.
  • In 2018, American Crime Story aired a season about Versace’s murder.
  • Today, the Versace brand is owned by Capri Holdings Limited (formerly Michael Kors Holdings).

    Who inherited Gianni Versace's fortune?

    In his will, Versace left 50% of his fashion empire to his niece, Allegra Versace. She and her younger brother, Daniel, inherited Versace’s rare artwork collection. Allegra inherited her stake, worth approximately $500 million, when she turned 18 in 2004.

    Does the Versace family still own Versace?

    Donatella Versace, whose late brother Gianni founded the company in 1978, will stay on as Creative Director. “It has been more than 20 years since I took over the company along with my brother Santo and daughter Allegra,” Donatella said in a statement.

    Why is Versace so famous?

    Versace became internationally renowned for ultra-glamorous creations as well as spectacular theatrical costumes and innovative menswear design. Versace’s style combined luxurious classicism with overt sexuality. His sensational advertising campaigns featured supermodels such as Linda Evangelista and Naomi Campbell.

    Who runs Versace now?

    The American designer’s holding company, Michael Kors Holdings, announced today the purchase of Italian label Versace for $2.12 billion.

    What is Versace worth today?

    Versace spent years building a name for his eponymous company, and even today, two decades after his death, his brand remains one of the most recognized labels in the fashion world. Now led by his sister Donatella, 62, the brand and company are currently valued at $1.7 billion, according to Reuters.
