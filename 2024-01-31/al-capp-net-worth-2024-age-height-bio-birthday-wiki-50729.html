Al Capp Biography

Al Capp is one of the most popular and richest Cartoonist who was born on September 28, 1909 in New Haven, Connecticut, United States. Cartoonist who is best known for his satirical, hillbilly-themed comic strip, Li’l Abner. His other comic strips include Abbie an’ Slats and Long Sam, and he was the 1947 recipient of the National Cartoonists Society’s Reuben Award for Cartoonist of the Year.

As a child, he enjoyed reading the literary works of Mark Twain.

He was married to Catherine Wingate Cameron from 1932 until his death in 1979. He had two biological daughters (Julie Ann and Catherine) and one adopted son (Colin).
