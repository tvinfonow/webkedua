Why did Chuck Howley win Super Bowl MVP?

He was also named most valuable player of Super Bowl V, after intercepting two passes and forcing a fumble in the Cowboys’ 16-13 loss to the Colts, making him the first defensive player and non-quarterback to receive the honor.

Is Chuck Howley in the NFL Hall of Fame?

Chuck Howley is a full-fledged Pro Football Hall of Fame finalist and one step away from being enshrined in Canton, Ohio. Howley, a Wheeling native and Warwood High graduate, was named as one of three selections out of the Hall’s seniors committee, joining defensive lineman Joe Klecko and cornerback Ken Riley.

Where did Chuck Howley play college football?

Howley played varsity football at West Virginia University in 1955, ’56, and ’57. He was third-team All-American in 1957 and then played in the East-West Shrine game, the Senior Bowl, and the College All-Star Game in Chicago.

Has a losing team ever had an MVP?

Coming to the Cowboys in a trade with the Chicago Bears in 1961 after once having given up on his NFL career, Chuck Howley became one of the greatest outside linebackers in Cowboys history and the only player to win Super Bowl MVP honors while on the losing team.
