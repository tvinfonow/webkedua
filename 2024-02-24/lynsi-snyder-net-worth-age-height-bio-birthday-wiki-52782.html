Lynsi Snyder Biography

Lynsi Snyder is one of the most popular and richest Businessperson who was born on May 5, 1982 in Glendora, California, United States. Snyder, who was 30 years old at the time, took over her father’s In-N-Out share (50%). She also inherited the remaining company assets that were not in her grandfather Esther’s trust. Snyder took full control of In-N-Out when she was 35.

Snyder was elected 6th president of In-N-Out on January 1, 2010. She succeeded Mark Taylor, her brother-in-law. Taylor was also appointed Chief Operating Officer. She holds the same position as her grandfather Harry (1948-1976), uncle Rich (76-1993), father Guy (93-1999) and grandmother Esther (999-2006). A taped message was recorded by Snyder before she became the President of In-N-Out Burger. It informed all associates about the transition and the future plans for the company. Three years after Esther Snyder’s passing, Lynsi Snyder replaced Esther Snyder’s signature on the paychecks of associates in 2009.

According to the Bloomberg Billionaires Index, in 2012, Snyder was the youngest American female billionaire. As the sole beneficiary of family trusts, she is currently In-N-Out’s majority owner and ultimately received control of the company’s stock in its entirety on her 35th birthday, becoming the sole owner.

In February 2013, Snyder was ranked a billionaire for the first time by the Bloomberg Billionaires Index, based on an In-N-Out valuation of $1.1 billion. That same year, she ranked #93 on Maxim’s annual Hot 100 list.

In-N-Out and Snyder were involved in a bitter suit with Rich Boyd, a former executive of the company, during 2006. Boyd was fired after he was accused of misusing company funds. But he claimed that Snyder and Mark Taylor were trying to expel Esther Snyder. Lynsi Snyder, Mark Taylor and the lawsuit were dismissed in May 2006.
