Gibbs was the one to lead Brown towards winning an Ivy League championship and the Elite Eight of the NCAA Tournament. He was awarded the Ivy League Player of the Year and an All-American First-Team selection. The year 2000 was the first time he played his off-season at collegiate in the Palm Beach Pumas of the Premier Development League. He was also among three Brown soccer players, which included Matthew Cross (Kansas City Wizards) and Scott Powers (Columbus Crew) and was selected during the 2001 MLS SuperDraft.

After graduation from Brown In 2001 Gibbs made the decision not take part in the MLS and instead joined FC St. Pauli of the Bundesliga. He played in 25 games with St. Pauli that season and became among the most youthful American to score goals in the Bundesliga by scoring with FC Cologne. He was also a part of the side that beat the world champions Bayern Munich, a result that earned St. Pauli the nickname of weltpokalsiegerbesieger, translated as World Club Championship Winner Beaters. St. Pauli was relegated to the 2. Bundesliga following the 2001-02 season and Gibbs played a similarly important role in the team’s his second season just as in his debut. St. Pauli, however was once again dropped from the league after the 2002-03 campaign and this time, into the Regionalliga Nord, the German third division. Gibbs continued to play for the club but was transferred from defensive defense to central midfielder.
