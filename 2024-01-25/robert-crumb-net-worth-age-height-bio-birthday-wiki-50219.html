Top Facts about Robert Crumb

  • Robert Crumb is an American cartoonist and illustrator born in 1943.
  • He created the iconic character Fritz the Cat in 1965.
  • Crumb’s work often features satirical social commentary.
  • His art style is characterized by intricate crosshatching and detailed linework.
  • In 1991, he was inducted into the Comic Book Hall of Fame.
  • Crumb has been a controversial figure due to his depictions of sex and race.
  • He contributed to underground comix publications like Zap Comix and Weirdo.
  • The documentary film “Crumb” (1994) explores his life and work.
  • Crumb’s brother Charles was also a notable artist before his death in 1997.
  • His artwork has been exhibited at major museums around the world, including the Museum of Modern Art in New York City.
