Angela Ballard (born 6 June 1982) is an Australian Paralympic athlete who competes in wheelchair sprints T53. She was diagnosed with paraplegia at the age of seven due to an accident in the car.

Ballard first started competing in race in a wheelchair in the year 1994 aged twelve. Her first racing wheelchair was purchased from an auction. In 1997, she was to take the sport more seriously and started breaking new records for Australian athletics for her category. The following year she represented her country on an international stage. In 2000, she had world records for the T53 100 and 200 m events.

In August 1998, Ballard competed at the International Paralympic Committee World Championships in Birmingham, England, where she was part of the gold medal winning Australian women’s 4 x100 m and 4×400 m relay teams. Both relay wins set long-standing world records. In the 2002 World Championships, she won gold in the 100 m. At the 2013 IPC Athletics World Championships in Lyon, France, she won silver medals in the Women’s 100 m, Women’s 200 m and Women’s 800 m T53 and a bronze medal in the 400 m T53.
