Top Facts about Shanice

1. Shanice Lorraine Wilson-Knox is an American singer, songwriter, actress and dancer.
2. She was nine years old when she appeared in a Kentucky Fried Chicken commercial with Ella Fitzgerald.
3. Her 1991 album, Inner Child, featured her best-known hit, “I Love Your Smile”, which reached the top ten in 22 countries, including the US Billboard Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs and No. 2 on the Billboard Hot 100.
4. She performed vocals on the tracks “Come on Over Here” and “Un-Break My Heart” for Toni Braxton, as well as “Bedtime” for Usher.
5. In 1997, she became the first Black performer to star in the role of Eponine in the musical Les Misérables on Broadway.
6. She married actor/comedian Flex Alexander on February 19, 2000 and they have two children.
7. She won two Grammy Awards and was nominated for 7 Soul Train Music Awards.
8. Her 1999 album, Shanice, featured the hit single “When I Close My Eyes”, which peaked at No. 12 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart.
9. While still with Motown, she recorded a cover version of “If I Were Your Woman” by Gladys Knight and the Pips.
10. She performed the English theme song for the video game The Bouncer, titled “Love Is the Gift”.
11. She starred in the made-for-TV movie One Special Moment in 2001.
12. She contributed to various film soundtracks, such as Boomerang (“Don’t Wanna Love You”) and The Meteor Man (“It’s for You”).
13. In 2010, she performed vocals on the track “Behind the Mask” for Michael Jackson’s posthumous album Michael.
14. She released her fifth studio album Every Woman Dreams in 2004 on her own label Imajah.
15. In 2022, she was selected to sing the theme song of the Sesame Street spinoff series Mecha Builders, alongside Eric Bellinger.
