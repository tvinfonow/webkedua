Top Facts about Alan Price

Key Facts:
1. Alan Price was born in Fatfield, Washington, County Durham.
2. He was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1994 as a member of the Animals.
3. He was a founding member of the Tyneside group the Alan Price Rhythm and Blues Combo, later renamed The Animals.
4. He left the Animals in 1965 due to personal and musical differences, as well as his fear of flying while on tour.
5. His hit singles with and without the Group include “Simon Smith and the Amazing Dancing Bear”, “The House That Jack Built”, “Rosetta” (with Georgie Fame), and “Jarrow Song”.
6. He recorded two albums with the Electric Blues Company featuring Bobby Tench and Zoot Money, the first was called Covers.
7. Price is known for work in film and television, taking occasional acting roles and composing the soundtrack to Lindsay Anderson’s film O Lucky Man!
8. He hosted shows such as musical Price To Play in the 1960s, featuring performances by Fleetwood Mac and Jimi Hendrix.
9. He composed and sang the theme song to the ATV series Turtle’s Progress in 1979.
10. He wrote the lyrics and music for Alfie Darling (1975) and The Whales of August (1987).
