Top Facts about Joanna Moore

  • Joanna Moore (née Dorothy Joanne Cook) was an American film and television actress from 1956 to 1986.
  • Moore had two children (Griffin O’Neal & Tatum O’Neal) with her ex-husband, actor Ryan O’Neal.
  • She was born Dorothy Joanne Cook in Americus, Georgia and was adopted at a young age.
  • Moore made her film debut in the 1957 crime drama Appointment with a Shadow and her television debut in the 1956 episode of Lux Video Theatre.
  • Her career peaked in the 1960s where she appeared in numerous television shows and 17 feature films.
  • She guest-starred on The Andy Griffith Show, Alfred Hitchcock Presents, Perry Mason and Bewitched.
  • In 1962 Moore suffered from otosclerosis and became deaf until an operation in 1962 restored her hearing.
  • From 1963 to 1967 she was married to her third husband, actor Ryan O’Neal.
  • Moore’s personal problems began to adversely impact her career in the 1970s.
  • After 1976 she landed only two minor film roles, Scout’s Honor and Run Chrissie Run!.
  • Moore was diagnosed with lung cancer in 1996 and died from it in 1997, twelve days after her 63rd birthday.
  • She was buried in Hillside Memorial Park Cemetery in Redlands, California and later moved to Oak Grove Cemetery in her home town of Americus, Georgia.
