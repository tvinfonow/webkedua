Marc Thiessen is one of the most popular and richest American author who was born on January 13, 1967 in United States. Thiessen was born January 13, 1967. Thiessen was born on Manhattan’s Upper East Side. His parents were both doctors and left-of-center liberal Democrat types. His mother was born in Poland, and he fought in the Warsaw Uprising as a teenager. This war ended in the death of his grandfather.

In 2001, he joined George W. Bush’s administration as Chief Speechwriter to Donald Rumsfeld. He was then promoted to Bush’s speechwriting staff in 2004. He was appointed chief speechwriter in February 2008 after William McGurn quit.

In March 2009, Thiessen and Peter Schweizer founded the communications firm, Oval Office Writers LLC.

Thiessen’s first book, Courting Disaster: How the CIA Kept America Safe and How Barack Obama Is Inviting the Next Attack, was published by Regnery Publishing in January 2010. In the book he argued that the CIA’s systematic use of enhanced interrogation techniques was effective, lawful, and moral. The book was endorsed by the former Vice President Dick Cheney, former Secretary of Defense Donald Rumsfeld, and former Attorney General Michael Mukasey. It reached the No. 9 spot on the New York Times Best Sellers list for hardcover nonfiction in February 2010.

Thiessen has been a Washington, D.C. resident for many years. He started his career at the lobbying firm Black, Manafort, Stone and Kelly. He was the Senate Foreign Relations Committee Chairman Jesse Helms’ senior policy advisor and spokesman on Capitol Hill from 1995 to 2001.
