Gerald Finley Biography

Gerald Finley is one of the most popular and richest Singer who was born on January 30, 1960 in Montreal, Quebec, Canada. Gerald Hunter Finley, OC CBE (born 30 January 1960) is a Canadian baritone singer from Canada.

Finley has been praised by critics in both concert and opera. Following a performance of his work at Carnegie Hall in March, 2007 he was described by New York Times critic Bernard Holland for being an “bass-baritone of easy luxury” and also that the “sensibilities begin with the pre-eminence of words.” His performance of Schumann’s Dichterliebe in the Wigmore Hall, in the year 2006 also was appreciated.

Finley debuted at Wiener Staatsoper as Count Almaviva in Le nozze di Figaro on 03 June 2012. He returned for lead roles in the premieres of two important new productions: the Forester in Janáček’s Příhody lišky Bystroušky in June 2014, and Amfortas in Wagner’s Parsifal in March 2017.

In October 2014 he opened the Canadian Opera Company 2014-2015 season as the title character in Falstaff. It was his return to the company after an absence of 20 years and his first appearance as Verdi’s Fat Knight.
