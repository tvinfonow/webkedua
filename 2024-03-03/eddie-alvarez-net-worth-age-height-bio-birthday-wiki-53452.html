Edward Alvarez (born January 11, 1984) is an American mixed martial artist who currently fights for ONE Championship. He formerly competed in the UFC and Bellator MMA, winning world titles in both respective promotions, having won the UFC Lightweight Championship once and the Bellator Lightweight World Championship twice. Alvarez is also known as being the first Lightweight champion in Bellator MMA history. He has also competed for the Japanese DREAM promotion, where he made his name in their inaugural Lightweight Grand Prix and also fought once for ProElite’s EliteXC promotion.

He went to Northeast Catholic High School, excelling and becoming a varsity letterman in football, wrestling and track. Despite receiving partial scholarship offers to wrestle in various colleges after graduating in 2001, he decided to pursue a career in mixed martial arts.

He has three sons, Eddie Jr., Anthony and Alister, with his wife Jamie Alvarez.
