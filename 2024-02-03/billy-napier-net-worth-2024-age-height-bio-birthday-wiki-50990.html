Napier was a star quarterback at Murray County High School in his hometown of Chatsworth, Georgia, where he played with his father, who was also his head coach. Napier was named All State in 1997 and received the offer of a football scholarship in the NCAA Division at Furman University. In his time with Furman’s Furman Paladins, Napier was a four-time letterwinner, and he took on the role of starting quarterback during his senior and junior seasons. The Paladins have won the conference title twice during his time with them and he was named on two All-Southern Conference teams at quarterback. In his senior year, he guided his group to win the NCAA Division 1-AA Football Championship and lost to Montana 13-6.

On December 15, 2017 Napier accepted the head coaching job of the Louisiana Ragin’ Cajuns. This is Napier’s first time as a head coach of a college football team. After arriving in December of 2017, his first season with the team resulted in a win of the western SBC division, and a bowl appearance (Cure Bowl losing to the Tulane Green Wave), resulting in an overall record of 7–7. Napier’s Cajuns defeated the Miami RedHawks in the 2020 LendingTree Bowl by the score of 27-17, his first bowl victory with the Cajuns and as a head coach.
