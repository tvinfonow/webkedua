Oscar Munoz (born January 5 1959) is an American businessman and executive chairman of United Airlines, having previously been the company’s chief executive. His time as the Chief Executive Officer for United Airlines began in September 2015. Prior to his appointment as CEO of United Airlines, Munoz served as a member of the board of the parent firm United Continental Holdings (UCH) and held executive posts in CSX Corporation and AT&T.

Munoz was employed by US West for five years before and briefly after the company was merged with Qwest. From 2001 to 2003 Munoz served as chief of finance and vice president of customer services for AT&T. Munoz was previously the regional vice-president of administration and finance at Coca-Cola Co. and held several financial positions at PepsiCo Inc. He joined CSX Corporation in 2003 as chief financial officer as well as chief of strategy. In 2012 the year he was elevated into chief operating officers at CSX and was in the position was his position until 2015 at which point he was promoted to president of the corporation. As Chief Operating Officer, Munoz had the responsibility of making sure that trains ran in time. In the middle of 2015, Munoz was appointed as the new CEO of CSX and officially took over the job at the close of the year. However, he was fired in September to become the CEO for United Airlines.

In 2015, Munoz was one of two Hispanic CEOs in the top 100 of the Fortune 500 list. Munoz has been named among the “100 Most Influential Hispanics” by Hispanic Business magazine. In March 2017, Munoz was named “Communicator of the Year for 2017” by PRWeek.
