Wayne Simien Biography

Wayne Simien is one of the most popular and richest Basketball Player who was born on March 9, 1983 in Leavenworth, Kansas, United States. Wayne Anthony Simien Jr. (born on March 9th 1983) is an American former professional basketball player who was last with the Spanish team Caceres Ciudad de Baloncesto. Simien was part of the Miami Heat team that won the 2006 NBA championship. Simien was a college player in the University of Kansas where he was a consensus first-team All-American during his senior year of 2005.

Simien was transferred from the Heat together alongside Antoine Walker and Michael Doleac to the Minnesota Timberwolves on October 24 on the 24th of October, 2007 in exchange in exchange for Ricky Davis and Mark Blount. On the 29th of October, Simien was waived by the Wolves to allow the team to remain at 15 players.

Simien decided to retire from professional basketball on May 5, 2009, to pursue work in Christian ministry.

At Kansas, Simien received All-American honors his junior and senior years. He was a Wooden Award finalist both years, and was the Big 12 Player of the Year his senior year. His college career ended when Kansas was defeated by 14th-seeded Bucknell in the first round of the 2005 NCAA Tournament. While at Kansas he won three Big 12 Championships and earned four NCAA Tournament berths including two Final Four appearances (one national runner-up finish) and one Elite 8 finish. Simien finished his college career with 110 wins and a 12–4 NCAA Tournament record. Simien was the Lowe’s Senior CLASS Award winner his final year, recognizing him as the nation’s top senior men’s basketball player. He finished his career as the 12th leading all-time scorer at Kansas with 1,593 points. Kansas retired his number 23 jersey on January 29, 2011.

Simien was not able to take part during the 2006 Heat’s Summer League program because of the salmonella infection. The season was eight games.
