Ariya Jutanugarn Biography

Ariya Jutanugarn is one of the most popular and richest Golfer who was born on November 23, 1995 in Bangkok, Thailand. Ariya Jutanugarn (Thai: e`riiyaa Cchuthaanukaal , the RTGS name is Eriya Chuthanukan, pronounced [?e:.ri:.ja: tcu?.tha:.nu?.ka:n] ; born 23rd November, 1995) is a Thai professional golfer who is a member of the US-based LPGA Tour. Her birthplace is Bangkok. It is her first time as a golfer, whether male or female who hails from Thailand to take home a major championship. She was ranked the top golfer in the Women’s World Golf Rankings in June 2017.

Jutanugarn has a ferocious and fearless style of playing. In 2013, at the Honda LPGA Thailand, she had a lead of two shots going to the last hole, but was beaten by just one stroke in Korean’s Inbee Park. She was 4th just a weeks later, at the HSBC Women’s Championships in Singapore. In the following weeks, she was the winner of her first professional event at the Let’s Lalla Meryem Cup in Morocco. This victory put her at the high on the LET Order of Merit (money list). She was the top player for in the opening two days of the Kingsmill Championship in Williamsburg, Virginia in May of 2013. She scored 7-under on the opening day.

At the 2016 Yokohama Tire LPGA Classic, Jutanugarn earned her first LPGA Tour win and became the first Thai winner on this tour. She went on to win the next two LPGA tournaments, thereby becoming the first player in LPGA history to win her first three titles in consecutive fashion. Jutanugarn won her first major championship with a three-stroke victory at the 2016 Women’s British Open.

Despite a mid-season slump in 2017 where Jutanugarn missed five cuts plus one withdrawal in a seven-tournament stretch, she still managed to win her 6th and 7th Tour titles. Her second Tour title of the year was the CME Group Tour Championship, the last event of the year, where she won $500,000 after finishing the weekend with back-to-back 67s. In addition to her two Tour wins, Jutanugarn racked up three runner-up finishes, a third place showing, and a total of 10 top-10 appearances which saw her win $1,549,858 and bringing her career total to $4,583,332. This was the second consecutive season where she earned more than $1,500,000.

At a practice course at 2013’s Wegmans LPGA Championship, Jutanugarn injured her shoulder when she fell down an ascent while running after her sister Moriya using the aid of a water bottle. The injury required surgery to correct it that was carried out in Bangkok.
