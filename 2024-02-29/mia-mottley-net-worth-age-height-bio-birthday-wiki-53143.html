Mia Mottley Biography

Mia Mottley is one of the most popular and richest Politician who was born on October 1, 1965 in Barbados. Her family has had a great influence on Mottley. Ernest Deighton Mottley (1907-1973), who was a successful real estate broker and politician, is her grandchild. He was the first Mayor of Bridgetown (1959), who represented Bridgetown in the House of Assembly since 1946. He belonged to the conservative party, and helped the poor. In June 1962, he was made Ordinary Commander in the Civil Division in Barbados for public services. He also assisted Wynter Algernon Croft (1910-1993), Barbados’s Trade minister, at the Independent Conference in London in June and July 1966.

Mottley completed her training as an attorney in 1986 and obtained a law degree at the London School of Economics.

Following the BLP’s victory in the 1994 general election, Mottley was appointed to the Minister of Education, Youth Affairs and Culture in September 1994, under Prime Minister Owen Arthur; at the age of 29, she became one of the youngest Barbadians ever to be assigned a ministerial portfolio. During her tenure she co-authored the White Paper on Education entitled Each Child Matters, which draws the link between better education and job fulfillment.

Mottley has been the Member of Parliament for the constituency of Saint Michael North East since 1994. From 1994 to 2008, she held a succession of ministerial portfolios including the post of Attorney-General of Barbados becoming the first woman to be appointed as such. She is also a member of the Inter-American Dialogue.

Ernest Deighton Mottley was Mottley’s uncle and became the leader of the Christian Social Democratic Party (CSD), which was founded in March 1975.
