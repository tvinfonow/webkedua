Scott Dunlap is one of the most popular and richest Golfer who was born on August 16, 1963 in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, United States. Scott Michael Dunlap (born August 16 1963) is an American professional golfer. He currently competes on the Champions Tour, having previously been a player on the PGA Tour.

Dunlap reached 50 in August 2013 and started playing on the Champions Tour full-time in 2014 and was the first winner during the Boeing Classic near Seattle that August, beating Mark Brooks on the first hole of a sudden death playoff. The 18th hole was par-5 and Dunlap’s shot on the second was stopped by at four feet (1.3 meters) away from his pin. Brooks birdie attempt was unsuccessful at thirty inches (9 millimeters) and Dunlap made two putts to victory. The winner’s share was $300,000. and, in addition to his trophy, Dunlap also received an official leather jacket.

Dunlap was in the Web.com Tour in 1990, 1998, and 2003 to 2013. He has two wins. He was as a player on the PGA Tour in 1996-97, 1999-2002 as well as 2012. His top results on PGA Tour were a trio of third places tied the 1996 Bell Canadian Open, 1999 Doral-Ryder Open, and 2000 The Players Championship and his top finish on the year-end cash list came in 44th position in the year 2000. He finished in the top ten in major championships, including The Open Championship in 1999 at Carnoustie as well as The PGA Championship in 2000 at Valhalla.
