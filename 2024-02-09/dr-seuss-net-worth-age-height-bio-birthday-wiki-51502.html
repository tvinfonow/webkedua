Dr. Seuss Biography

Dr. Seuss is one of the most popular and richest Children's Author who was born on March 2, 1904 in Springfield, Massachusetts, United States. American author and cartoonist who wrote 46 immortal children’s books under the pen name of Dr. Seuss. His most notable works include The Cat in the Hat (1957), How the Grinch Stole Christmas (1957), and Green Eggs and Ham (1960).

In 2008, Geisel’s induction into the California Hall of Fame was announced by Arnold Schwarzenegger.

He left Lincoln College, Oxford before receiving his PhD in English literature, having met his future wife and fellow children’s author, Helen Palmer, who convinced him to pursue a career in illustration. During his marriage, he had an affair with Audrey Stone Dimond, who became his second wife following Helen Palmer Geisel‘s 1967 suicide.

Did Dr. Seuss actually have a PHD?

Seuss earned a bachelor’s degree from Dartmouth College in 1925 and did some postgraduate studies in literature at Lincoln College, Oxford, and at the Sorbonne, but he did not earn a doctorate. He then became an illustrator and humorist for magazines before landing a career in advertising.
